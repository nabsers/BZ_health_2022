Lifestyle Habits of the World’s Healthiest, Longest-Lived People


The term ‘bluezone’ is used to describe five regions across the world where residents live much longer than the average mortality rate across the world. According to a research that identified the island of Sardinia as a location in which there was the greatest concentration of male centenarians (over 100 years old), and further discovered that there are five regions across the world where people not only live the longest, but are also the healthiest. These bluezone locations are, Sardinia, Italy; Okinawa, Japan; Ikaria, Greece; Nicoya, Costa Rica and Loma Linda, CA USA.

NHS UK recommends Mediterranean diet that accommodates traditional healthy living habits of people from the Mediterranean. It has been a known fact that Mediterranean diet is healthy, and two of the identified blue zones are located in that Mediterranean region. The research on to the bluezones also have identified that within each of these zones, there were nine specific lifestyle habits that were synonymous with this concentrated longevity.

Power 9 lifestyle habits

Residents living in the bluezones areas share nine healthy lifestyle habits that help them live longer, healthier lives.

The blue zone regions of the world share and benefit from a set of behaviours and habits that are called power 9 practises to increase longevity, happiness, and good health in the following ways

  • Move naturally: Our bodies are machines and work best when used regularly
  • Purpose driven life: Waking up each day with a outlook or purpose for your life
  • Downshift: Reduce stress
  • 80% Rule: Enjoy Your meals, but stop eating when you are 80% full
  • Plant based food: Eat more plant-based foods while limiting meat consumption
  • Wine at 5: Wine in moderation
  • Belong to a community: A sense of belonging
  • Family and loved ones: Put your family first
  • Right tribe: Surround yourself with the right people
